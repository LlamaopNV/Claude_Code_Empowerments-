The headline result

Where it started, what it became

The tournament kept idea-forge's adversarial, carry-forward soul but rebuilt the machine around it. The winning direction came from contender #3, "minimize feasibility and cost risk."

idea-forge v1BEFORE
Mechanism
  • Single-elimination bracket, 8 to 4 to 2 to 1, seven debates.
  • Each debate: two exchanges, a judge, a carry-forward edit, about 6 calls per pair.
  • The winner absorbs the loser's strongest objection, then advances.
The cracks it shipped with
  • The carry-forward edit was asserted, never audited.
  • The thing it shipped, the accumulated survivor, was never measured against anything.
  • A run of clean wins could still hand you a chimera quietly worse than one of its parents.
  • Cost: about 24 to 30 Opus calls per run.
idea-forge v2AFTER
Mechanism
  • King-of-the-hill ladder: a pre-screened best seed holds the hill; a frozen copy goes to reserve.
  • Each rung: one gated Opus call (max three), steelman to scored verdict to salvage to merge to audit.
  • The champion is a running merge that compounds every compatible fix.
The guarantees it now carries
  • A graft ledger re-validates every merge against all prior grafts; conflicts are resolved, not buried.
  • A predecessor audit rolls back any merge that regresses the champion.
  • A final audit rung: ship only if provably no worse than the best original, else ship the original.
  • Cost: about 18 to 24 Opus calls (lite mode about 9 to 12).
The forge's own summary of the change

"v1 calibrated the selector carefully but the carry-forward edit was asserted, never audited, and the one thing it shipped was never measured. v2 keeps the adversarial, divergent, carry-forward soul but audits the synthesizer as hard as the selector: each graft is re-validated against a composable ledger and must match-or-beat the previous champion or roll back, and a mandatory audit rung ships the champion only if it provably clears a calibrated margin over the reserved original. The result is cheaper per round and carries a real guarantee v1 lacked: a champion provably no worse than the best original."

The tournament that did it

Eight rival designs entered. One walked out.

Each contender attacked v1 from a different angle. They debated in pairs; a judge scored each on five criteria and quoted the deciding line; the winner absorbed the loser's strongest objection before advancing. Contender #3, the one obsessed with cutting cost and feasibility risk, won the final.

Final gauntlet, the last attack the champion had to survive
"The apparatus certifies every pairwise verdict to the hilt but never once evaluates the thing it actually ships, the accumulated champion. You can win all rungs by clearing margins and still ship a chimera worse than the best single original, because the load-bearing claim is the one quantity the system never measures."

The champion was then amended to answer exactly this: the predecessor audit, the graft ledger, and the final reserve audit rung all exist because this objection landed. That is reinforcement under selection, working on the skill itself.

The nine upgrades

Every change, traced to the objection that forced it

This is the actual changelog the synthesis step emitted, not a summary written after the fact.

1Bracket to king-of-the-hill ladder

Replaced the single-elimination bracket with a ladder whose advancing artifact is a running merge that compounds every compatible fix; the champion only ever accumulates and is never wholesale-replaced.

driven by: the gauntlet's path-dependence and unaudited-synthesizer objection

2Pre-screen seeding plus frozen reserve

One cheap Opus pass ranks the 8 originals, names the strongest as champion-zero, and freezes a pristine copy in reserve, so incumbency accrues to the a-priori-best, not to spawn order.

driven by: open risk, incumbency was handed to a random first mover

3One gated call per rung

Each round is a single gated call returning steelman, scores with verbatim rubric quotes, winner with margin and self-consistency retry, salvage, merge, and predecessor audit, cutting cost from about 6 calls per debate to 1 to 3 per rung.

driven by: the winning contender's cost mandate

4The graft ledger plus composable compatibility

Every new graft must assert non-contradiction against each live ledger entry; a flagged conflict forces a resolve-edit that keeps the stronger graft and updates the ledger, instead of silently accumulating contradictions.

driven by: riskiest-assumption, compatibility may be non-monotonic

5Whole-artifact predecessor audit plus rollback

Any non-no-op merge is re-judged against the immediately previous champion; a regression is independently confirmed and rolled back, costing the graft but not the recorded verdict. Selection and synthesis are decoupled.

driven by: core gauntlet objection, the synthesizer was never audited

6Mandatory audit rung against the reserve

The accumulated champion ships only if it clears the noise floor over the pristine original, otherwise the original ships. "Better than the best original" becomes a measured gate and a hard floor.

driven by: "the system never measures the thing it ships"

7Noise-floor calibration

Margin of 2 or more auto-certifies; a verdict inside the roughly 1-point band triggers one fresh position-swapped judgment with the prior verdict hidden. Agree certifies, disagree means incumbency holds. Applied to ladder rungs and the audit rung.

driven by: judge-reliability risk on near-ties

8Cost folded into the rubric

Strengthened axis 3 and the feasibility criterion to "feasibility and cost," with explicit verbatim rubric lines the judge must quote for every score.

driven by: the winning contender axis

9Honest claims, not slogans

Updated framing, red flags, cost numbers, lite-mode axes, worked example, and checklists to claim only "no worse than the best original, hardened by every surviving graft," never a global optimum, and to name the residual path-dependence.

driven by: residual path-dependence open risk
Was it effective?

The honest scorecard

Three categorical wins, and three limits the skill names about itself rather than hides.

A new guarantee appeared

v1 could ship something worse than its own inputs. v2 cannot: the reserve audit rung is a provable floor. That is a categorical, not cosmetic, gain.

It got cheaper while getting safer

About 24 to 30 down to 18 to 24 Opus calls per run, with a 9 to 12 lite mode, even as three new audit mechanisms were added.

Every fix traces to an attack

All nine changes map to a specific objection raised and survived in the bracket. The mechanism did what it claims to do, on itself.

Same-model echo chamber

All eight contenders and every judge share a model family. The debate's diversity is real but capped. v2 names this and flags mixing model families as future work.

Order still shapes the outcome

A running merge is path-dependent. The reserve caps the downside but a different seeding could compound a different set. v2 claims a floor, not an optimum, deliberately.

Quality is argued, not benchmarked

"Better" here is an LLM-judge verdict, not an external eval. The next honest step is an A/B against a flat generate-8-pick-1 ensemble.
